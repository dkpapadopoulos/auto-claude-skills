# Gate-Gaming Follow-up: detector limits + validated deferred-item adoptions

## Why

A research cycle (goal: "prove the deferred items and revalidate the gate-gaming detector with tests")
produced three evidence-backed outcomes. The method: a labeled corpus (26 cases) plus **two blind
held-out sets** generated by other agents, benchmarked with an independent harness.

1. **The "improved" detector (v3) was overfit and must NOT ship.** An assertion-count + tautology +
   strong→weak candidate scored F1 **0.93 in-sample** but **0.14 held-out — worse than the shipped
   line-grep detector (0.44)**. A second candidate (v4: comment-exclusion + broadened skip dialects)
   beat shipped on held-out-1 (0.44→0.70) but **tied it on a fresh held-out-2 (0.64=0.64)** — its
   gains were distribution-specific. Conclusion: **more regex does not robustly close the gap; the
   line-grep approach has a real coverage ceiling.** Decision: escalate beyond regex (future work),
   and ship honesty now.
2. **batch-scripting silent-failure gap is real** (demonstrated runnably): `claude -p` exits 0 while
   writing empty output; the OK-log + final test suite miss it for non-test-covered targets.
3. **outcome-review lacks a failure-cause split** (A/B confirmed): current guidance risks reverting a
   working feature whose tracking broke, or discarding an under-rolled-out one.

## What Changes

- **project-verification:** add a **Limits** note to the gate-gaming detector — `clean` is not proof;
  state the measured F1 range and the structural blind spots (subject-stub, control-flow, block/
  docstring-muted asserts, uncommon skip dialects) and false-alarm classes (moves/renames). No
  detector code change (v3/v4 rejected on held-out evidence).
- **batch-scripting:** Step 3 gates the `OK` log on a per-file **postcondition** (non-empty AND
  changed AND a cheap parse/sanity check), not the `claude -p` exit code alone.
- **outcome-review:** add a required **Cause** field for any non-`Confirmed` hypothesis
  (instrumentation-broken / adoption-gap / product-miss / inconclusive-data), with recommendations
  following from cause.

## Capabilities

- **Modified:** `project-verification` — detector limits are stated (trust calibration).
- **Modified:** `pdlc-closed-loop` — outcome-review classifies failure cause before recommending.
- (batch-scripting has no behavioral capability of its own; the postcondition is impact-only.)

## Impact

- `skills/project-verification/SKILL.md`, `skills/batch-scripting/SKILL.md`, `skills/outcome-review/SKILL.md`
- `tests/test-project-verification.sh`, new `tests/test-batch-scripting-content.sh`, `tests/test-outcome-review-content.sh`
- Stacks on `feature/gate-gaming-detection` (PR #79): the limits note annotates the detector shipped there.

## Out of Scope

- Any change to the gate-gaming detector regex (v3 and v4 both failed blind held-out validation).
- The robust ceiling-breaker (per-change **coverage-delta** or an **LLM-judge over the test diff**) —
  scoped as future work with the blind-held-out protocol as its adoption gate. Revival trigger: a
  recorded instance where a `clean` detector verdict masked real gate-gaming that coverage-delta
  would have caught.
- batch retry-classification (killed earlier — contradicts the skill's own no-backoff anti-pattern);
  agent-team-review inter-lens disagreement (dissent already survives the severity floor; unvalidatable
  in the single-turn runner).
